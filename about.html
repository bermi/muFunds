µFunds

Author: José Ballester

Last version: v1.3 (19 November 2017)

µFunds lets you import your mutual fund NAVs and other attributes from Morningstar to your spreadsheet by using a simple formula.

Usage

Insert this formula in a cell:

=muFunds(option, isin)

where isin is your mutual fund ISIN (for European funds) or ticker (for American funds) and option is one of the following options:

Options

nav: Net Asset Value (NAV) of your mutual fund, i.e. last price of the fund's shares.

change: NAV variation between last price and the previous one, in percentage (%).

date: Date for last NAV.

currency: Currency for displayed NAV.

expenses: Expense ratio from Morningstar.

category: Fund category from Morningstar.

style: Fund investment style from Morningstar.

Examples

European funds

Checking last NAV for Bestinfond (ES0114673033) Spanish mutual fund:

=muFunds("nav", "ES0114673033")

American funds

Checking last NAV for Vanguard Total Stock Market Index Fund (VTSMX) mutual fund:

=muFunds("nav", "VTSMX")

Considerations

In specific cases, outputs from µFunds may be considered as strings rather than numbers. To avoid problems (e.g. for conditional formatting), you can call the µFunds function combined with the value function:

=value(muFunds("change", "VTSMX"))
